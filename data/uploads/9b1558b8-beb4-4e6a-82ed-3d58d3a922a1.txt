ARTICLE 3 — TIME

3.1  DATE OF COMMENCEMENT

The date of commencement of the Work shall be May 1, 2023,
the date of the Owner's Notice to Proceed.

3.2  SUBSTANTIAL COMPLETION

The Contractor shall achieve Substantial Completion of the
entire Work not later than August 15, 2024, subject only to
adjustments in the Contract Time as provided in the General
Conditions.

3.3  LIQUIDATED DAMAGES

In the event the Contractor fails to achieve Substantial
Completion by the date specified in Section 3.2, as such date
may be adjusted by Change Order, the Contractor shall pay the
Owner liquidated damages in the amount of Four Thousand Five
Hundred Dollars ($4,500.00) per calendar day of delay, up to a
maximum aggregate of Six Hundred Thousand Dollars ($600,000.00).

The parties agree that actual damages resulting from delay
would be difficult to calculate and that the liquidated damages
set forth herein are a reasonable estimate of such damages and
do not constitute a penalty.


ARTICLE 8 — CHANGES IN THE WORK

8.1  CHANGE ORDERS

No change in the Work, whether by way of alteration, addition,
or deduction, and no extension of the Contract Time, shall be
made except pursuant to a written Change Order signed by the
Owner, the Architect, and the Contractor.

8.2  NOTICE OF CLAIM FOR ADDITIONAL TIME OR COMPENSATION

8.2.1  If the Contractor wishes to make a Claim for an increase
in the Contract Sum or an extension of the Contract Time, the
Contractor shall give the Owner and the Architect written notice
of such Claim within fourteen (14) calendar days after the
occurrence of the event giving rise to the Claim or within
fourteen (14) calendar days after the Contractor first recognizes
the condition giving rise to the Claim, whichever is later.

8.2.2  The Contractor's failure to provide timely written notice
as required by Section 8.2.1 shall constitute a waiver of the
Claim. The Owner shall have no obligation to consider any Claim
for which timely notice was not provided.

8.2.3  Notice under this Section shall be delivered in writing to
the Owner's designated representative and to the Architect.
Verbal notice, emails not specifically identified as a notice of
claim, and notations in daily logs shall not satisfy the notice
requirements of this Section.


ARTICLE 9 — OWNER-CAUSED DELAYS

9.1  OWNER RESPONSIBILITY

If the Contractor is delayed at any time in the commencement or
progress of the Work by any act or neglect of the Owner or the
Architect, by changes ordered in the Work, or by other causes
which the Contractor, in its reasonable judgment, determines
justify delay, then the Contract Time shall be extended by Change
Order for such reasonable time as the Architect may determine.

9.2  NO DAMAGES FOR DELAY

Notwithstanding any other provision of this Agreement, the
Contractor shall not be entitled to monetary damages for delay
in the performance of the Work, except as expressly provided by
written Change Order. The Contractor's sole remedy for delay
shall be an extension of the Contract Time.

Provided, however, that this Section 9.2 shall not apply to
delays caused by the Owner's active interference with the
Contractor's work or the Owner's bad faith failure to perform
obligations under this Agreement.


ARTICLE 12 — DISPUTE RESOLUTION

12.1  STEP NEGOTIATION

Prior to commencing any legal action, the parties shall attempt
to resolve any dispute through direct negotiation between senior
representatives of each party.

12.2  MEDIATION

If direct negotiation is unsuccessful, the parties shall submit
the dispute to non-binding mediation before a mediator mutually
agreed upon.

12.3  VENUE

Any legal action arising out of or relating to this Agreement
shall be brought in the Circuit Court of the Fifteenth Judicial
Circuit in and for Palm Beach County, Florida.
