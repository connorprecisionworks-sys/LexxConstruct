Dear Mr. Vance:

This letter constitutes formal written notice of claim by
Keystone Building Group, Inc. ("Keystone") under Article 8 of
the Contract dated April 12, 2023, between Palmetto Ridge
Development, LLC ("Owner") and Keystone for the Palmetto Ridge
Medical Office Building project.

Keystone hereby claims entitlement to (1) an extension of the
Contract Time of ninety-four (94) calendar days and (2)
additional compensation in the approximate amount of
$1,847,000.00, subject to further analysis and documentation,
for delays and impacts caused by the Owner and the Architect
as more fully described below.


I.   BACKGROUND

Keystone commenced work under the Contract on May 1, 2023
pursuant to the Owner's Notice to Proceed. The Contract
established a Substantial Completion date of August 15, 2024,
representing a 471-day construction duration.

As of the data date of Keystone's Schedule Update No. 11
(February 29, 2024), the project is 94 calendar days behind the
baseline schedule, with a current forecast Substantial Completion
date of November 17, 2024. Keystone's analysis, supported by
contemporaneous project records, demonstrates that the
substantial majority of this delay is attributable to causes
for which the Owner bears responsibility under the Contract.


II.  BASES OF CLAIM

A.   LATE RESPONSES TO CRITICAL-PATH REQUESTS FOR INFORMATION

Contract Section 8.2 requires the Architect to respond to
Requests for Information within seven (7) calendar days. During
the period from July 2023 through February 2024, the average
response time to RFIs submitted by Keystone was twenty (20)
calendar days, nearly three times the contractual requirement.

Eight critical-path RFIs experienced response delays of 13 to
28 days each. The most significant individual impact was
RFI-089 (Level 3 mechanical room structural conflict), which
received a response 28 days after the required date, suspending
steel erection at Level 3 from October 20, 2023 through
November 16, 2023.

Cumulative critical-path delay attributable to RFI response
delays: approximately 34 calendar days.

B.   OWNER-DIRECTED DESIGN CHANGES

Between December 2023 and February 2024, the Owner issued
three significant design change directives that impacted the
critical path:

  1. December 4, 2023 - Conversion of Level 2 shell space to
     imaging suite
  2. January 8, 2024 - Revision of elevator specifications from
     hydraulic to traction
  3. January 22, 2024 - Relocation of primary electrical service
     entrance

These changes required redesign work, subcontractor re-mobilization,
and re-sequencing of dependent trades. Change Order proposals
COP-017, COP-019, and COP-021 addressing these changes remain
unresolved.

Cumulative critical-path delay attributable to owner-directed
changes: approximately 42 calendar days.

C.   DELAYED OWNER-FURNISHED EQUIPMENT

The Contract contemplates owner-furnished installation of
medical imaging equipment on Levels 1 and 2. The Owner's
procurement of this equipment has not proceeded in accordance
with the baseline schedule assumptions, and current vendor
delivery estimates reflect an 8-10 week delay.

Cumulative critical-path delay attributable to owner-furnished
equipment delays: approximately 18 calendar days.


III. MITIGATION EFFORTS

Keystone has undertaken substantial acceleration efforts at
its own cost to mitigate the impacts described above, including
extended work shifts, additional equipment mobilization, and
subcontractor overtime authorization. These efforts have
recovered approximately 22 calendar days of schedule time, but
are insufficient to offset the cumulative delay impacts
caused by the Owner.
