QUESTION:
================================================================

There is a conflict between the architectural detail shown on
Sheet A8.02 (Detail A-501) and the waterproofing manufacturer's
published installation requirements for the balcony-to-wall
transition at the typical unit balconies on floors 11 through 18.

Specifically:

1. Detail A-501 shows the waterproofing membrane terminating at
   the face of the CMU backup wall with a 4-inch upturn and
   sealed with a termination bar.

2. The manufacturer's published installation guide requires
   the membrane to be installed over a minimum 6-inch upturn
   onto the wall substrate, with the substrate prepared and
   primed per the manufacturer's specifications, and the
   termination to be covered by the subsequent exterior finish
   system with no exposed termination bar.

3. Additionally, the manufacturer requires concrete substrates
   to achieve a minimum 28-day cure OR verified 75% of design
   compressive strength, whichever occurs first, prior to
   membrane application. The architectural specifications do
   not address substrate cure requirements.

Please provide direction on:

(a) Which detail should govern the installation - the
    architectural detail or the manufacturer's requirement?

(b) Confirmation of the required substrate cure time prior to
    membrane application.

(c) If the manufacturer's detail governs, please issue a
    revised detail so we can update our installation sequence
    and coordinate with Coastal Waterproofing Systems.

We are currently proceeding with balcony slab pours on the
lower floors of this sequence and will need a response before
waterproofing work begins on floors 11-14, currently scheduled
to start the week of August 8, 2022.

Signed: Raymond Castillo
        Superintendent, Meridian Construction Group

================================================================
ARCHITECT'S RESPONSE:
================================================================

Response Date:  June 13, 2022
Respondent:     Margaret Harlan, AIA, Harlan Architecture + Design

Response:

The manufacturer's installation requirements shall govern where
they conflict with or exceed the architectural details. This is
consistent with Specification Section 07 14 16, Paragraph 1.4,
which states that manufacturer's written installation
instructions are part of the contract documents and shall be
followed.

Specifically:

(a) The manufacturer's detail (6-inch minimum upturn with
    concealed termination) shall govern. Detail A-501 is
    superseded as to the termination method. A revised detail
    will be issued as part of the next drawing package; in the
    interim, the contractor shall proceed in accordance with
    the manufacturer's published installation guide.

(b) Concrete substrate cure requirement is confirmed: minimum
    28-day cure OR verified 75% of design compressive strength,
    whichever occurs first, shall be achieved prior to membrane
    application. Verification by cylinder break results shall
    be documented and retained in the project quality records.

(c) Revised detail will be issued under separate cover. Do not
    delay balcony waterproofing work pending the revised detail;
    proceed per manufacturer's guidelines.

Contractor is directed to coordinate with Coastal Waterproofing
Systems to confirm their installation procedures are consistent
with the manufacturer's requirements before commencing work on
floors 11 and above.

Signed: Margaret Harlan, AIA, Principal
        Harlan Architecture + Design
