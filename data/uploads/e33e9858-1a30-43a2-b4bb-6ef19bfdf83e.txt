Palmetto Ridge Development, LLC ("Owner") is in receipt of
Keystone Building Group, Inc.'s ("Keystone") Notice of Claim
dated March 22, 2024. The Owner has reviewed the Claim with
the Architect and the Owner's construction counsel. For the
reasons set forth below, the Owner denies the Claim in its
entirety.


I.   THE CLAIM IS UNTIMELY AND THEREFORE WAIVED

Contract Section 8.2.1 requires Keystone to submit written
notice of any Claim within fourteen (14) calendar days after
the occurrence of the event giving rise to the Claim, or
within fourteen (14) days after Keystone first recognizes the
condition, whichever is later. Section 8.2.2 provides that
failure to provide timely notice constitutes a waiver of the
Claim.

Keystone's March 22, 2024 Notice of Claim references events
dating back to July 2023, including RFI response delays and
design coordination issues. The notice period for those events
expired long before March 22, 2024. Specifically:

  - The RFI-089 response was received by Keystone on November
    14, 2023. Any claim related to that RFI was required to be
    submitted by November 28, 2023. Keystone's March 22, 2024
    submission is 115 days late with respect to this claim.

  - The owner-directed changes Keystone now complains of were
    issued on December 4, 2023, January 8, 2024, and January
    22, 2024. Notice deadlines for those events were December
    18, 2023, January 22, 2024, and February 5, 2024,
    respectively. All deadlines passed without Keystone
    providing the required notice.

Contract Section 8.2.3 is explicit that verbal notice, emails
not identified as notices of claim, and notations in daily logs
do not satisfy the notice requirement. Keystone's contemporaneous
project documentation contains no formal Notice of Claim for
these events prior to March 22, 2024.

The Claim is therefore waived in its entirety under the plain
language of Section 8.2.2.


II.  THE CLAIM LACKS SUPPORT ON THE MERITS

Without waiving the timeliness defense set forth above, the
Owner also denies the Claim on the merits.

A.   PROJECT SCHEDULE STATUS

The Owner disputes Keystone's characterization of the project
as 94 calendar days behind schedule. Based on the Owner's
review with the Architect, the actual delay as of February 29,
2024 is approximately 55 calendar days, the majority of which
is attributable to Keystone's own performance issues, including
but not limited to:

  - Inadequate staffing levels during the summer 2023 framing
    sequence
  - Quality issues with Level 2 concrete pours requiring
    partial remediation
  - Subcontractor coordination failures documented in Architect's
    Field Observation Reports

B.   RFI RESPONSE TIMES

The Owner acknowledges that some RFI responses exceeded seven
days, but notes that many of the RFIs submitted by Keystone
were deficient, incomplete, or requested information that was
available in the contract documents. RFI response time, when
measured against properly submitted RFIs, averages within
contractual requirements.

With respect to RFI-089 specifically, the Owner's position is
that the underlying design conflict should have been identified
during Keystone's shop drawing review process, which is a
contractual obligation under the General Conditions.

C.   OWNER-DIRECTED CHANGES

The design changes referenced in the Claim were either (1)
requested by Keystone to address constructability issues, or
(2) minor scope refinements that should not have impacted the
critical path. The Owner is prepared to negotiate reasonable
change order amounts for legitimate additional work, but
rejects Keystone's characterization of these changes as
delay-causing owner actions.

D.   NO-DAMAGES-FOR-DELAY PROVISION

Contract Section 9.2 expressly bars monetary damages for
delay, providing that the contractor's sole remedy for delay
is an extension of Contract Time. Keystone's claim for
$1,847,000 in delay damages is directly contrary to this
provision. The narrow exception for "active interference" and
"bad faith" does not apply; the Owner has acted in good faith
throughout the project.


III. OWNER'S POSITION ON SCHEDULE AND LIQUIDATED DAMAGES

The Owner's position is that Keystone is responsible for any
delay beyond the Contract Substantial Completion date of
August 15, 2024. In the event Keystone fails to achieve
Substantial Completion by that date, the Owner will assess
liquidated damages at the contractual rate of $4,500 per
calendar day, up to the $600,000 maximum, and will pursue
all other remedies available under the Contract.


IV.  NEXT STEPS

The Owner is willing to meet with Keystone to discuss the
pending Change Order Proposals (COP-017, COP-019, and COP-021)
and to address legitimate contract questions. However, the
Owner will not negotiate the Claim as presented, which is both
untimely and unsupported.

If Keystone wishes to pursue the Claim further, the Owner
will rely on the dispute resolution provisions of Article 12
and its contractual and legal rights.
