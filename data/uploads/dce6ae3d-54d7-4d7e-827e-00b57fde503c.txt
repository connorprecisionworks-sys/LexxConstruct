                       P R O C E E D I N G S

     Thereupon,

                       RAYMOND CASTILLO,

having been first duly sworn, was examined and testified
as follows:

                       DIRECT EXAMINATION

BY MS. RUIZ:

     Q.   Good morning, Mr. Castillo. Could you please state
your full name for the record.

     A.   Raymond Arturo Castillo.

     Q.   And what is your current occupation?

     A.   I'm a senior superintendent with Meridian Construction
Group. I've been with the company about eleven years.

     Q.   Prior to Meridian, where did you work?

     A.   I was with Turner Construction for about six years
before that, mostly on commercial high-rise projects in South
Florida.

     Q.   And what is your educational background?

     A.   I have an associate's degree in construction
management from Miami Dade College. I also have an OSHA 30
certification and I'm a certified concrete field testing
technician through ACI.

     Q.   Have you ever testified in a deposition before today?

     A.   Yes, ma'am. Three times, I believe. All construction
matters.


                                                                   Page 4


     Q.   Let's turn to the Bayshore Tower project. What was
your role on that project?

     A.   I was the superintendent of record from the start of
construction through substantial completion. I was on site
every day the crew was working.

     Q.   When did construction begin?

     A.   We mobilized in February of 2021. Foundations started
the following month.

     Q.   And when was substantial completion?

     A.   November 18, 2023. I remember because it was two days
before Thanksgiving.

     Q.   Mr. Castillo, are you familiar with the balcony slab
issues that are the subject of this lawsuit?

     A.   Yes, I am.

     Q.   When did you first become aware that there were
problems with the balcony slabs?

     A.   Uh, I would say probably around August of 2022. We
were doing the balcony pours on floors eleven through fourteen
during that period.

     Q.   What did you observe?

     A.   There was some question about whether the waterproofing
membrane was being installed in accordance with the
manufacturer's specifications. I noticed that the Coastal
Waterproofing crew was applying the membrane before the
concrete had fully cured in some areas.

                                                                   Page 5


     Q.   Did you report this observation to anyone?

     A.   I mentioned it to the project manager, Tom Brennan.

     Q.   Verbally?

     A.   Yes, verbally. I also noted it in my daily log.

     Q.   Did you write up a non-conformance report?

     A.   No, I did not.

     Q.   Why not?

     A.   Because Tom told me he would handle it with Coastal's
foreman directly. He said it wasn't necessary to escalate it
formally.

     Q.   And did the issue get resolved?

     A.   I was told it was. I didn't personally verify the
corrections.

     Q.   So you didn't inspect the remediation work?

     A.   Not specifically, no. I continued with my other
supervisory duties.

     Q.   Mr. Castillo, as the superintendent of record, wasn't
it your responsibility to verify that identified defects were
corrected?

          MR. HALPERN: Objection. Form. Calls for a legal
     conclusion.

          MS. RUIZ: You can answer.


                                                                   Page 6


     A.   In general, yes, that would be part of my duties.
But in this case I relied on Tom's assurance that it had been
handled.

     Q.   Let me show you what I'm going to mark as
Plaintiff's Exhibit 1.

          (Plaintiff's Exhibit 1 marked for identification.)

BY MS. RUIZ:

     Q.   Mr. Castillo, do you recognize this document?

     A.   Yes. That's my daily log from August 12, 2022.

     Q.   Is this the log in which you noted the waterproofing
issue you just described?

     A.   Yes, ma'am.

     Q.   Can you read for the record the entry near the bottom
of the page under "Issues Observed"?

     A.   It says, "Coastal applying membrane on floors 12 and
13 before concrete cure time met. Notified T. Brennan."

     Q.   And what is the date written at the top of this log?

     A.   August 12, 2022.

     Q.   Thank you. Now, earlier you testified that you first
became aware of the balcony slab issues in August 2022, is
that correct?

     A.   That's right.


                                                                   Page 7


     Q.   Mr. Castillo, I'd like you to turn to what's been
marked as Plaintiff's Exhibit 2.

          (Plaintiff's Exhibit 2 marked for identification.)

BY MS. RUIZ:

     Q.   Do you recognize this document?

     A.   Yes. That's RFI number 47.

     Q.   Who prepared this RFI?

     A.   I did.

     Q.   And what is the date on this RFI?

     A.   June 3, 2022.

     Q.   June 3, 2022. And what was the subject of this RFI?

     A.   It's asking the architect for clarification on the
waterproofing detail at the balcony-to-wall transition. There
was a conflict between the architectural drawings and the
waterproofing manufacturer's recommended detail.

     Q.   So as of June 3, 2022, you were aware that there was
an issue with how the waterproofing was supposed to be
installed at the balconies?

     A.   I was aware of a detail conflict. That's different
from knowing there was a defect in the work itself.

     Q.   Mr. Castillo, you testified a few minutes ago that
you first became aware of the balcony slab issues in August of
2022. Is that testimony still accurate?


                                                                   Page 8


     A.   I mean, the detail question came up in June, but the
actual installation issue I observed was in August. Those are
two different things in my mind.

     Q.   Did you inform the project manager about the June
RFI?

     A.   Tom was copied on the RFI when it went out, yes.

     Q.   And did you receive a response?

     A.   The architect responded about ten days later directing
us to follow the manufacturer's detail.

     Q.   So by mid-June 2022, you had a written directive to
follow the manufacturer's installation requirements?

     A.   Correct.

     Q.   And yet in August you observed Coastal violating those
very requirements?

          MR. MORETTI: Objection. Form. Argumentative.

     A.   I observed what appeared to be a deviation, yes.

     Q.   Let me show you Plaintiff's Exhibit 3.

          (Plaintiff's Exhibit 3 marked for identification.)

BY MS. RUIZ:

     Q.   These are photographs taken by the owner's consultant
in March of 2024. Do you see the condition of the balcony slab
at Unit 1204?


                                                                   Page 9


     A.   I can see the photographs, yes.

     Q.   Can you describe what you see?

     A.   There's visible water intrusion staining on the
underside of the slab and what looks like efflorescence along
the edge.

     Q.   Based on your experience, what causes that condition?

     A.   Typically that's caused by water migrating through the
slab or through a failed waterproofing membrane.

     Q.   Mr. Castillo, is the condition shown in these
photographs consistent with a properly installed waterproofing
system?

     A.   No. A properly installed system should not allow that
kind of water migration.

     Q.   Thank you. Let me ask you about scheduling. Were the
balcony pours on floors eleven through fourteen ahead of
schedule or behind schedule when they occurred?

     A.   We were behind. Maybe three weeks behind the baseline.

     Q.   And was there pressure from Meridian's management to
accelerate the work?

     A.   There's always pressure on a project like this. I
wouldn't call it unusual.

     Q.   Was there a specific directive to catch up on schedule?

     A.   Tom told the crews to work six tens instead of five
eights during that period, yes.


                                                                  Page 10


     Q.   And do you believe the accelerated schedule contributed
to Coastal's crew applying waterproofing before the concrete
had properly cured?

          MR. HALPERN: Objection. Form. Calls for speculation.

     A.   I think the schedule pressure was a factor, yes. When
you're trying to make up time, sometimes corners get cut. I'm
not saying that's what happened for sure, but it's possible.

     Q.   Mr. Castillo, let me show you what's been marked as
Plaintiff's Exhibit 4.

          (Plaintiff's Exhibit 4 marked for identification.)

BY MS. RUIZ:

     Q.   This is Change Order No. 11. Do you recognize it?

     A.   Yes.

     Q.   What does this change order cover?

     A.   It's for additional waterproofing work on the
balconies on floors eleven through fourteen. Remediation work.

     Q.   And what is the date?

     A.   October 4, 2022.

     Q.   So approximately six weeks after you observed the
improper installation, Meridian was issued a change order for
remediation work on the very same balconies?

     A.   That appears to be correct.


                                                                  Page 11


     Q.   Who requested this change order?

     A.   Tom Brennan on behalf of Meridian.

     Q.   And what was the amount?

     A.   I believe it was around eighty-seven thousand dollars.

     Q.   Mr. Castillo, is it fair to say that Meridian charged
the owner for remediation work that was necessary because of
defective installation by a subcontractor?

          MR. HALPERN: Objection. Form. Mischaracterizes the
     evidence. Calls for a legal conclusion.

     A.   I wouldn't characterize it that way. I don't know all
the details of the change order negotiation. That was handled
above my level.

     Q.   But the physical remediation work was necessary, was
it not?

     A.   Yes, the work was performed.

     Q.   Because the original installation was defective?

     A.   Because there were concerns about the installation,
yes.

     Q.   Mr. Castillo, you have been testifying today that the
installation issue was something Tom Brennan was handling. Do
you have any documentation of Mr. Brennan's instructions to
Coastal Waterproofing to correct the installation?


                                                                  Page 12


     A.   I don't personally have that documentation, no. That
would be in the project management files.

     Q.   Have you searched for it?

     A.   I haven't been asked to.

     Q.   To your knowledge, does any written record exist of
Mr. Brennan directing Coastal to correct the installation?

     A.   I'm not aware of one.

     Q.   Did you ever speak directly with Luis Ordoñez, the
Coastal foreman?

     A.   A few times, in passing. Not about this specific issue.

     Q.   So to summarize: you observed what you believed to be
improper waterproofing installation, you reported it verbally
to your project manager, you did not write a non-conformance
report, you did not personally verify the remediation, and you
are not aware of any written record of remediation instructions
being given to the subcontractor. Is that an accurate summary?

     A.   That's accurate, yes.

     Q.   Mr. Castillo, in your eleven years as a superintendent,
have you ever before failed to document a known installation
defect with a non-conformance report?

          MR. HALPERN: Objection. Form. Argumentative.

     A.   Not that I recall, no.


                                                                  Page 13


     Q.   So this was the first time?

     A.   I suppose so, yes.

     Q.   Why?

     A.   I relied on Tom. That was my mistake.

     Q.   Thank you, Mr. Castillo. I have no further questions
at this time.

          MR. HALPERN: I have a few questions on cross.

                       CROSS-EXAMINATION

BY MR. HALPERN:

     Q.   Mr. Castillo, Mr. Halpern for Meridian Construction.
Just a few quick clarifications. When you observed the
waterproofing installation in August of 2022, you are not a
waterproofing expert, correct?

     A.   Correct.

     Q.   You were relying on general construction knowledge?

     A.   Yes.

     Q.   And it is possible that what you observed was not
actually a defect, correct?

     A.   It's possible, I suppose.


                                                                  Page 14


     Q.   Mr. Brennan was your supervisor on the project?

     A.   Yes.

     Q.   And it was reasonable for you to rely on his judgment
regarding how to handle the issue?

     A.   I thought so at the time.

     Q.   Nothing further.

          MR. MORETTI: A few questions for Coastal.

                       CROSS-EXAMINATION

BY MR. MORETTI:

     Q.   Mr. Castillo, Anthony Moretti for Coastal Waterproofing.
You never personally inspected any of the waterproofing membrane
after installation, did you?

     A.   Not specifically, no.

     Q.   So when you say the installation was defective, you
are basing that on what you saw during installation, not on
post-installation inspection?

     A.   Correct.

     Q.   And you don't know whether Coastal returned to correct
the areas in question?

     A.   I don't know personally, no.

     Q.   Nothing further.


                                                                  Page 15


          MS. RUIZ: Brief redirect.

                     REDIRECT EXAMINATION

BY MS. RUIZ:

     Q.   Mr. Castillo, earlier you testified that the remediation
work covered by Change Order No. 11 was performed. How do you
know it was performed if you did not personally inspect it?

     A.   Because I signed off on the change order payment
application. Payment was made, so the work must have been
done.

     Q.   So your knowledge that the remediation occurred is
based on a payment record, not on physical inspection?

     A.   Yes, that's fair.

     Q.   Nothing further.

          MR. HALPERN: Nothing further from Meridian.
          MR. MORETTI: Nothing from Coastal.
