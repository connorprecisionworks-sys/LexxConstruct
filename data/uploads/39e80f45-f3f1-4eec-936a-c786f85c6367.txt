EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
================================================================

The project is currently 94 calendar days behind the baseline
schedule as of the February 29, 2024 data date. The projected
substantial completion date has moved from August 15, 2024
(baseline) to November 17, 2024 (current forecast).

The primary drivers of schedule slippage are: (1) delayed
responses to critical-path RFIs by the design team, (2) owner-
directed design changes to the Level 3 mechanical layout, and
(3) late delivery of owner-furnished medical imaging equipment
that impacts interior finishes sequencing.

Keystone Building Group reserves all rights under the Contract,
including rights to additional time and compensation for
owner-caused delays, subject to compliance with the notice
provisions of Contract Section 8.2.

================================================================
PRIMARY DELAY DRIVERS THIS PERIOD
================================================================

1. RFI RESPONSE DELAYS

During the January-February 2024 period, the project received
responses to 6 critical-path RFIs. Average response time was
23 calendar days, against the contractual 7-day requirement
(see Contract Section 8.2).

The most significant individual impact was RFI-089 (Level 3
Mechanical Room structural conflict), which received a response
28 days after the required date. Steel erection on Level 3 was
suspended from October 20, 2023 through November 16, 2023
pending direction. Follow-on trades were delayed through
December.

Cumulative impact from RFI delays during this update period:
approximately 34 calendar days of critical path delay.

2. OWNER-DIRECTED DESIGN CHANGES

The owner issued three significant design change directives
during this period:

  - December 4, 2023: Conversion of Level 2 shell space to
    imaging suite (added scope, required MEP redesign)
  - January 8, 2024: Revised elevator specifications from
    hydraulic to traction (impacted shaft construction)
  - January 22, 2024: Relocation of primary electrical service
    entrance (impacted site utilities sequencing)

Cumulative impact from owner-directed changes during this
period: approximately 42 calendar days of critical path delay.

Change Order proposals for these changes are in various stages
of negotiation. COP-017, COP-019, and COP-021 remain unresolved
as of this data date.

3. OWNER-FURNISHED EQUIPMENT DELAYS

The Owner has not yet finalized procurement of the following
owner-furnished equipment that must be in place prior to
adjacent finish work:

  - MRI machine (Level 1 imaging suite)
  - CT scanner (Level 1 imaging suite)
  - Two ultrasound units (Level 2 imaging suite)

Current vendor estimates indicate delivery in June-July 2024,
approximately 8-10 weeks later than the baseline assumed.

Cumulative impact: approximately 18 calendar days (estimated,
pending confirmation of delivery dates).


================================================================
RECOVERY EFFORTS
================================================================

Keystone has implemented the following acceleration measures at
its own cost to mitigate schedule slippage:

  - Extended crew shifts (6x10 schedule) for steel and concrete
    trades beginning November 13, 2023
  - Additional concrete pump mobilized to allow two simultaneous
    pour locations
  - Added second tower crane operator to extend hoisting hours
  - Subcontractor overtime authorized for MEP rough-in trades

Despite these efforts, the recovered time (approximately 22
days) is insufficient to offset the cumulative delay impacts
identified above.


================================================================
RESERVATION OF RIGHTS
================================================================

This schedule update documents schedule impacts attributable to
causes beyond Keystone's control. Keystone specifically reserves
all rights under the Contract, including:

  - Right to additional Contract Time pursuant to Article 9
  - Right to additional compensation for owner-caused delays,
    including active interference and bad faith failures, to
    the extent the no-damages-for-delay provision of Section
    9.2 does not apply
  - Right to recover acceleration costs incurred in recovery
    efforts
  - Right to challenge the assessment of liquidated damages
    for delays not caused by Keystone

A formal Notice of Claim covering these matters will be
submitted under separate cover in accordance with Contract
Section 8.2.
