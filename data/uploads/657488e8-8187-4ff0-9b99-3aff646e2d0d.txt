QUESTION:
================================================================

During the framing of Level 3, our steel erection crew has
identified a significant conflict between the structural
drawings (Sheet S-301, Detail 4) and the MEP coordination
drawings (Sheet M-302) at the Level 3 mechanical room, Grid E-7.

Specifically:

1. The structural drawings show a W24x68 beam at elevation
   42'-6" along gridline E between grids 6 and 8, with no
   openings or penetrations indicated.

2. The MEP drawings (Sheet M-302) require a 36" x 48"
   rectangular duct penetration through this exact location,
   along with a 12" chilled water supply/return pair and a 6"
   condensate drain, all at the elevation where the structural
   beam is located.

3. Physical installation of the MEP systems as currently drawn
   is not possible without either (a) relocating the structural
   beam, which requires structural redesign, or (b) rerouting
   the entire Level 3 mechanical distribution, which requires
   MEP redesign.

This conflict affects the critical path of the project. Steel
erection on Level 3 cannot proceed past gridline E until this
is resolved. Subsequent trades (MEP rough-in, framing, fireproofing)
are also held up.

Please provide direction immediately. Every day of delay in
responding to this RFI directly impacts the project critical
path.

Signed: Jorge Medina, Project Manager, Keystone Building Group


================================================================
ARCHITECT'S RESPONSE (Received November 14, 2023):
================================================================

After coordination with the structural engineer (Vantage
Structural, P.A.) and the MEP engineer (Ellison MEP Consulting),
the following resolution is directed:

1. The structural beam at gridline E-7 shall be modified to
   a W27x84 with a reinforced opening to accommodate the MEP
   penetrations. Revised structural drawings (S-301R1) are
   attached.

2. The chilled water piping shall be re-routed along gridline F
   per revised MEP drawings (M-302R1) attached.

3. The condensate drain routing remains as originally shown.

4. The contractor is directed to proceed with the revised
   design. Any associated cost or time impacts shall be
   addressed by separate Change Order upon the contractor's
   submission of a detailed impact analysis.

We acknowledge that the delayed response to this RFI may have
contributed to schedule impacts. However, we note that this
design coordination issue was not identified during the shop
drawing review phase, and the contractor's obligation to review
and coordinate trade drawings under the General Conditions
remains in effect.

Signed: Daniel Whitfield, AIA
